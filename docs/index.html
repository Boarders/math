Geometric universes and topoi

A geometric universe $E$ is defined to be a cartesian closed category equipped with a subobject classifier. A morphism $f : E \to F$ of geometric universes is given by a functor $f^*:F\to E$ preserving finite limits equipped with a right adjoint $f_*: E\to F$. A 2-morphism $f\to g$ is given by a natural transformation $f^*\to g^*$.

Let $E$ be a geometric universe. A $E$-topos $X$ or a topos over $E$ is defined to be a geometric universe $\Sh{X}$ equipped with a structure map $X:E\to\Sh{X}$ whose gluing fibration $\GL{X}\to{E}$ has a generating family. A morphism of $E$-topoi $f:{X}\to{Y}$ is defined by a morphism $\Sh{f}:\Sh{Y}\to\Sh{X}$ of geometric universes equipped with a 2-isomorphism $\phi_{f}:\Sh{f}\circ Y\cong X$ in $[E,\Sh{X}]$.
